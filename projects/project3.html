Design and Analysis of Centrifugal Pump Impeller

Objective

To design and analyze a centrifugal pump impeller by optimizing material selection and impeller structure. Focus was given to achieving better strength-to-weight ratios, improving corrosion resistance, and reducing failure risks under high-pressure underwater applications.

What Was Done

  • Designed a semi-open type impeller using Autodesk Fusion 360 based on calculated pump parameters.
  • Performed structural analysis (stress, strain, deformation) and modal analysis (natural frequencies) using ANSYS 2021.
  • Compared Grey Cast Iron and Glass Fiber Reinforced Plastic (GFRP) materials for better performance and longevity.
  • Simulated centrifugal pump setup using SolidWorks to validate the design functionality under realistic flow conditions.

Challenges

  • Finding the optimal material balancing corrosion resistance, strength, and manufacturability.
  • Ensuring structural safety under rotating loads and hydro-dynamic forces.
  • Minimizing deformation and avoiding resonance-induced failures through modal analysis.

What Was Learned

  • Advanced 3D modeling and simulation for turbomachinery applications.
  • Material properties like density, tensile strength, and vibration behavior influence real-world pump efficiency.
  • CFD and FEA integration provides reliable performance validation even before physical prototyping.

What Was Achieved

Successfully demonstrated that GFRP outperforms Grey Cast Iron for centrifugal impellers with 65% weight reduction, improved structural stability, and better vibration resistance. The optimized design ensures longer life for centrifugal pumps operating in harsh environments like marine applications.
