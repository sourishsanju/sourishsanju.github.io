Quadcopter Team Configurable Motion Guided by a Quadruped

Objective

To design a cooperative motion strategy where a quadruped robot guides the motion of a team of quadcopters using affine deformation modeling, improving multi-agent path coordination and adaptive search capabilities.

What Was Done

  • Modeled quadcopter team motion using affine transformation guided by quadruped motion paths.
  • Integrated ROS2 and Vicon system for real-time localization and communication among agents.
  • Developed adaptive configuration updating strategies to maintain optimal drone formation under path constraints.
  • Experimentally validated with quadcopters and a Unitree Go1 quadruped using Vicon-based motion capture.

Challenges

  • Maintaining stable multi-agent configurations during non-uniform quadruped motion.
  • Minimizing path drift and deformation lag between leader and follower robots.
  • Implementing synchronized communication without network congestion or data loss.

What Was Learned

  • Affine deformation modeling for dynamic team motion coordination.
  • ROS2 multi-node communication architecture for real-time robotic control.
  • Handling synchronization challenges between aerial and ground robots using Vicon.

What Was Achieved

Successfully demonstrated that quadruped-guided drone formations can dynamically adjust their configuration in real-time while maintaining coverage and formation constraints. Published results in a leading robotics conference.
