Design and Fabrication of Mini Ball Milling Machine

Objective

To design and fabricate a compact mini ball milling machine for efficient grinding of solid materials into fine powders, focusing on laboratory and prototyping use, especially for metal specimen preparation.

What Was Done

  • Designed the mini ball mill structure using SolidWorks and AutoCAD 3D models.
  • Fabricated key components like jar, shaft, pulley, and base frame using stainless steel and mild steel.
  • Integrated a 5HP, 1450 RPM induction motor with a pulley-belt system for smooth jar rotation.
  • Created a cascading ball effect inside the jar using strategically placed lifter bars.

Challenges

  • Ensuring minimal vibration and shaft misalignment during operation.
  • Optimizing RPM to balance grinding efficiency without overheating.
  • Managing energy losses and friction at shaft-bearing interfaces.

What Was Learned

  • Understanding the dynamics of cascading and cataracting effects for particle grinding.
  • Material selection strategies balancing strength, machinability, and corrosion resistance.
  • Importance of motor-load matching and system vibration damping techniques.

What Was Achieved

Successfully fabricated a fully operational mini ball milling machine capable of grinding steel and metallic powders with improved time efficiency. Experimental tests validated the cascading mechanism’s ability to enhance grinding performance, providing a reliable lab-scale solution for fine powder preparation.
