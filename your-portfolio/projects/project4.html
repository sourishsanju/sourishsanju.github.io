Energy Consumption Prediction Using Machine Learning

Objective

To build accurate predictive models for HVAC energy consumption using ML techniques such as XGBoost, CatBoost, LGBM, and Linear Regression — enabling reduction in carbon emissions from building systems.

What Was Done

  • Processed a dataset with 20M+ records from over 1000 buildings, incorporating weather and building metadata.
  • Performed exploratory data analysis, feature extraction, and missing value handling for better model training.
  • Implemented CatBoost, XGBoost, LGBM, and Linear Regression models for energy prediction tasks.
  • Conducted hyperparameter tuning to optimize model performance and minimize errors.

Challenges

  • Managing a huge dataset and optimizing computation for 20M+ rows.
  • Feature selection and missing value imputation without information leakage.
  • Preventing model overfitting and ensuring generalization across different sites.

What Was Learned

  • Importance of integrating environmental (weather) data for better energy prediction accuracy.
  • Comparison and strengths of various ML models, finding XGBoost to outperform others in RMSE and MAE.
  • Role of hyperparameter tuning and early stopping in preventing overfitting in regression models.

What Was Achieved

Successfully demonstrated that advanced gradient boosting models significantly outperform traditional regression methods for energy prediction. XGBoost achieved the best prediction accuracy, helping optimize energy efficiency efforts.
