Motion Planning for Quadruped Teams Using a Dynamic Fluid Flow Model

Objective

To develop a novel, collision-free motion planning method for multi-quadruped systems using a dynamic fluid flow model, enabling safe navigation in both obstacle-laden and dynamic environments.

What Was Done

  • Designed a dynamic fluid flow field model where robots follow streamlines to avoid obstacles and agents.
  • Implemented real-time control architecture using ROS2, Vicon motion capture, and Unitree Go1 quadrupeds.
  • Conducted experiments validating motion planning across different scenarios (static and dynamic).

Challenges

  • Maintaining collision-free paths in a crowded multi-agent environment.
  • Real-time adjustment of trajectories based on moving obstacles and groups.
  • Integrating high-fidelity Vicon tracking with live ROS2 agent control.

What Was Learned

  • Applied ideal fluid flow models to multi-agent motion planning effectively.
  • Learned real-time system control combining motion capture with robotic agents.
  • Demonstrated experimental robustness of dynamic obstacle avoidance using stream-based trajectories.

What Was Achieved

Developed and validated a scalable, dynamic fluid-based navigation method for quadruped teams, showing excellent collision avoidance in both static and dynamic environments. Research successfully presented at ICARCV 2024.
