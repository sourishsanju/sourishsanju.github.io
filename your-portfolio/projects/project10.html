Generative Design and Simulation of GE Engine Bracket

Objective

To use simulation-driven generative design to optimize a GE engine bracket for strength, weight reduction, and manufacturability, applying real-world loading scenarios.

What Was Done

  • Imported GE bracket model into Autodesk Fusion 360.
  • Applied realistic constraints and multiple load cases (1.5x min, 0.75x max, etc.).
  • Performed material optimization using Titanium, Cobalt Chrome, and Aluminum.
  • Simulated stress, strain, and displacement behavior to validate designs.
  • Generated CNC-compatible G-code after evaluating additive manufacturing suitability.

Challenges

  • High stress concentration at sharp corners leading to design failure under load.
  • Managing post-processing costs and tolerance errors in additive manufacturing.
  • Balancing lightweight design with sufficient mechanical robustness for jet engine environments.

What Was Learned

  • Importance of geometry in cost, scrap rates, and failure prediction.
  • Generative design techniques for multi-material performance optimization.
  • Real-world manufacturing integration through Fusion 360 G-code simulation.
  • Critical trade-offs between mass reduction, stress tolerance, and manufacturability.

What Was Achieved

Selected Titanium 6Al-4V as the ideal design material, achieving a high strength-to-weight ratio. Reduced mass by 20% while maintaining required safety factors under extreme load conditions. Successfully generated manufacturing-ready models with optimized cost vs performance balance.
